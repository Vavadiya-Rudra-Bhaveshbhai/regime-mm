# References

## Core Papers

1. **Avellaneda & Stoikov (2008)**
   *High-frequency trading in a limit order book*
   Quantitative Finance, 8(3), 217–224.
   → The foundation. Derives the HJB PDE for a market maker with Brownian price and Poisson order flow.

2. **Cartea, Jaimungal & Penalva (2015)**
   *Algorithmic and High-Frequency Trading*
   Cambridge University Press.
   → Best textbook. Chapter 4–6 cover market making, inventory risk, and stochastic control.

3. **Cartea & Jaimungal (2013)**
   *Modelling asset prices for algorithmic and high-frequency trading*
   Applied Mathematical Finance.
   → Extends A-S with alpha signals and order flow imbalance.

## Regime-Switching

4. **Hamilton (1989)**
   *A new approach to the economic analysis of nonstationary time series and the business cycle*
   Econometrica, 57(2), 357–384.
   → Original regime-switching model for macroeconomics; the discrete-time precursor.

5. **Elliott, Aggoun & Moore (1995)**
   *Hidden Markov Models: Estimation and Control*
   Springer.
   → Reference for continuous-time HMM filtering and the Wonham filter derivation.

## HMM Filtering

6. **Wonham (1964)**
   *Some applications of stochastic differential equations to optimal nonlinear filtering*
   SIAM Journal on Control, 2(3), 347–369.
   → Original paper deriving the continuous-time Bayesian filter used in Phase 4.

7. **Liptser & Shiryaev (2001)**
   *Statistics of Random Processes II*
   Springer.
   → Rigorous treatment of the innovations approach and nonlinear filtering.

## Stochastic Control

8. **Fleming & Soner (2006)**
   *Controlled Markov Processes and Viscosity Solutions*
   Springer.
   → HJB equations, viscosity solutions, and verification theorems.

9. **Øksendal (2003)**
   *Stochastic Differential Equations: An Introduction with Applications*
   Springer (6th ed.)
   → Best intro to Itô calculus, Brownian motion, and SDEs.

## Market Microstructure

10. **Glosten & Milgrom (1985)**
    *Bid, ask and transaction prices in a specialist market with heterogeneously informed traders*
    Journal of Financial Economics, 14(1), 71–100.
    → The adverse selection model: why spreads exist.

11. **Kyle (1985)**
    *Continuous auctions and insider trading*
    Econometrica, 53(6), 1315–1335.
    → Information asymmetry and the lambda (price impact) parameter.
